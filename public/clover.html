Where are Göttingen's four-leaf clovers?

PhD student from Göttingen single-handedly drives local four-leaf clover population to the verge of extinction.

2h ago - By MUNE SAUTE


Something you might not see in a long time if you live in or near Göttingen: a four-leaved clover.


Göttingen (dpo) - "To me it's just something that I do." claims Jan Doof* (*surname has been anonymized by the editorial department).
"Whenever I stray out into the concreted wilderness that people call Göttingen *stops to appreciate his choice of words* I strife to find yet another 4 leaf clover to add to my collection."
Collection. That is the term under which Jon Daw refers to his mausoleum of clovers.
"It takes one hell of a vigorous/disciplined man to find four leaf clovers these days.", Jan continues with a boasting smile on his face.

"Sometimes I even forget about picked ones"

Jan Doof. 28. More of a clover himself than a gardener.
But who is this person that so relentlessly picks every 4 leaf clover he comes across.

4-leafed clover have always been behind in numbers compared to their rather ordinary 3-leafed siblings. "We cannot let humans destroy what nature has been working on for millenia." says Kranu Meye, head and founder of the 'Make-Trees-Not-War'-Initiative Göttingen. Biologist Schucy Leelen confirms that the extra leave offers the immense energy-advantage of up to 133%. "People like Mr. Doof here are trying to stop evolution. They hide their fear of change behind arrogance and self-rightousness." On a more direct note, she adds: "Also: Mr. Doof, you make it sound like finding 4-leaf-clovers is particularly hard. Well, to be honest, they are much easier to find than their 3-leaf-neighbours. Up to 33% easier to be precise."

Doof doesn't seem to care.

"What they gonna do? Sue me?", Jan smiles as he turns away.
